Voice and Tone

"If it sounds like writing, I rewrite it." — Elmore Leonard

Choose Energy's voice is human and familiar, and our tone is conversational. Think of us as the well-informed friend or neighbor you always turn to for sound advice.

We are:

  • Helpful but not salesy
  • Friendly but not intrusive
  • Casual but not lazy
  • Authoritative but not pushy
  • Confident but not cocky
  • Smart but not stodgy
  • Knowledgable but not jargony

Our unofficial tagline is "empowering choice," and that spirit should come through in every piece of content we create.

Writing Style

To borrow from the late great George Orwell:

  • Never use a long word where a short one will do.
  • If it is possible to cut a word out, always cut it out.
  • Never use the passive where you can use the active.
  • Never use a foreign phrase, a scientific word, or a jargon word if you can think of an everyday English equivalent.
  • Break any of these rules sooner than say anything outright barbarous.

Grammar and More

  • We heart the Oxford comma. Please use a comma after the penultimate item in a list of three or more items, before 'and' or 'or.'
  • Please use bulleted or numbered lists whenever possible.
  • Feel free to use contractions
  • "To be" or not to be... please avoid "to be" verbs whenever possible, they can almost always be replaced by a more precise and active verb.
    • We would not say: Bill is going to start bringing all of his books to the group session for studying.
    • We would say: Bill decided to bring all of his books to the group study session.
  • Sometimes a prepositional phrase is necessary. If you can find a way to cut out a prepositional phrase (to, of, from, about, in, on, etc.) do so.
  • Avoid suspended sentences. Avoid subordinate clauses that break up the flow of the sentence. Those clauses should be placed at the beginning of the sentence if necessary.
