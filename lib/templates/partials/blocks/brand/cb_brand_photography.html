Photography Guide

  • Through our photography, we want to provide a human face and emphasize the human side of energy use to allow people to feel better connected to the unfamiliar experience of switching energy providers.
  • Personalize the user experience as much as possible by using location-specific imagery
  • Use modern, relatable imagery

Art Direction

  • Photography should all be Landscape orientation, i.e. no Portrait or Square.
  • Avoid use of fire of any type that is not natural gas, i.e. no logs, no fireplaces, no bonfires, fireworks, etc.
  • Ensure every person in a photograph exudes positivity, i.e. smiling or laughing people.
  • Unless the photos provide major location context, avoid people who are participating in activities unrelated to using some form of energy, i.e. people laying in the grass, sitting on a dock, or similar. Instead, focus on people who are participating in activities related to energy use, i.e. people cooking on a gas stove (ensure the burners are lit), people watching TV/movies, people using computers/technology, people spending time together indoors.
  • Avoid grayscale photography.
