Introduction

About Choose UX

Choose UX is a living document, versioned in GitHub. ChooseUX/master should be considered the canonical branch. If you are going to make changes to the style guide, please make your changes in your own branch and use a pull request to get it into ChooseUX/master.

Designs and interactions in Choose UX are not always meant for copy and paste implementation into other products. They are meant to be a representation of what the resultant interface should look like or how it should function.

Questions or comments should be submitted on GitHub Issues.

Design Philosophy

  • Comforting

    • Switching energy providers is a confusing and scary process for for many people. Our goal should be to demystify in a comforting manner.

    • Providing a human face and emphasizing the human side of energy use allows people to feel better connected to the unfamiliar experience of switching energy providers.

  • Device Agnostic

    • We are designing a web product that can be used on numerous devices. Our products should be device agnostic and adapt to work on any device in our ecosystem.

    • Design different interactions optimized for different input mechanisms (i.e. touch-tap vs mouse-click) not device type (i.e. phone vs desktop).

  • Directional

    • Everything in our products should be designed with a specific direction. We should be driving people towards specific goals with everything in the product.

    • As a shopping experience, our core goal as a business is to drive conversions. From a practical perspective, this should be the primary direction which every person using our product should be heading.

  • Educating

    • Confusion abounds when people try to switch their energy suppliers. Providing the education people need when they need it is a core competency in which we should excel.

    • Our products often need lots of explanation. Distilling every explanation into an easy-to-learn overview and a deeper, richer educational experience is important for converting confusion to confidence.

  • Modern

    • Energy choice is a very modern concept. We should be echoing this in our interfaces and forward-thinking products.

    • Our products should evoke feelings of living in the future without relying on futuristic tropes. Use active and bleeding-edge design trends as inspiration.

  • Modular

    • Our product should aim to build small, modular blocks of content and interactions. We want the ability to mix and match modules throughout all of our products.

    • When thinking about components we are designing and building, aim for the smallest meaningful atomic unit so that we may have the ability to reuse it somewhere else.

  • Simple

    • Our competitors use obfuscation as a weapon to keep customers beholden to them. We should be the exact opposite: empower people by clarifying things with plain, easy-to-understand language and imagery.

    • Our user interfaces should parallel the easy-to-understand language of our site. Make it simple for anyone to use our products.

  • Trustworthy

    • Providing a unified, consistent experience from end-to-end is important for building trust with consumers who are dealing with multiple businesses (utility co., energy supplier, and energy marketplace) for every transaction.

    • Engendering trust with social proof, data visualization, and trusted third-party content will be important in helping people through the switching process.

Color Usage

Typically, we will be focusing on a lighter, monochrome interface with colors providing highlights for interaction points through our primary brand colors. Our primary brand colors are Shamrock Green, Curious Blue, and Sun Orange.

Shamrock Green

Shamrock Green is our primary interaction color. Green is typically a color associated with positive interactions and growth. It should be the most commonly used color throughout our site, as it will be the default color for call-to-action buttons and default text link color.

Example usages include the following:

  • default CTA button color
  • default link color

Curious Blue

Curious Blue is our primary informational color. Blue is typically a color associated with information and calm. Generally, this color should designate informational content. In terms of frequency of use, this should be the least used of our three brand colors.

Example usages include the following:

  • non-CTA button color
  • non-CTA icon color
  • informational label color
  • informational tooltips

Sun Orange

Sun Orange is our secondary interaction color. Orange is typically a color associated with action and excitement. When using our orange palette, be careful not to overuse it in any given interface view, as its saturation and strength as a call-to-action may become distracting.

Example usages include the following:

  • CTA icon color
  • active link color
  • alert or CTA label color

Typography

Headlines for Choose Energy. Strong. Emphasis.

We use Franklin Gothic URW Demi for our headlines. The grotesque flourishes on Franklin Gothic like in the "g" and the cut of various descenders provides a distinct feel.

@include typeface-headline() or $font-family-headline + $font-weight-headline
Subheaders for Choose Energy. Strong. Emphasis.

Like our primary headlines, we use Franklin Gothic URW for our subheads; however, instead of the Demi weight, we use the Book weight for our subheads in order to provide appropriate contrast between our headlines.

@include typeface-subhead() or $font-family-subhead + $font-weight-subhead
Accent text for Choose Energy. Strong. Emphasis.

For accent copy like pull quotes and blockquotes, we use Merriweather Regular. It provides a nice contemporary serif that works well with the more modern feel of the body copy.

@include typeface-accent() or $font-family-accent + $font-weight-accent
Body copy text for Choose Energy. Strong. Duis mollis, est non commodo luctus, nisi erat porttitor ligula, eget lacinia odio sem nec elit. Emphasis. Etiam porta sem malesuada magna mollis euismod. All the things.

For body copy and user interface text, we use Proxima Nova Light, a modern geometric typeface with limited flourishes to contrast with the headlines.

@include typeface-body() or $font-family-body + $font-weight-body

Content Structures

Editorial Block

The editorial block is a bit unique in its construction. There is a standard .content-editorial component, but it generally will require a context to be useful. Each of the editorial blocks below has a different context, listed beforehand. Typically, these will be art-directed and delivered as completed HTML and CSS for implementation.

It should also be noted that every editorial block has content set to the $site-max-width but expands well beyond that at 100% width for its background image.
